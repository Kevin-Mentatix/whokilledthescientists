BODY FOUND Melissa Casias remains found in Carson National Forest — handgun recovered alongside body

A hiker discovered human remains on May 28 in the McGaffey Ridge area of Carson National Forest, southeast of Taos. New Mexico State Police confirmed on May 30 that the remains have been positively identified as Melissa Casias, the 53-year-old Los Alamos National Laboratory employee missing since June 26, 2025. A handgun was found alongside the remains. Cause and manner of death have not yet been determined — the remains will undergo further anthropological examination by the Office of the Medical Investigator. When Casias disappeared, her daughter found both her personal and work cell phones at home wiped of all data, along with her wallet, keys, and car. Her husband Mark Casias also worked at LANL. This is the first body recovery among the New Mexico missing cluster.

PLUTONIUM DEAL DOE selects 5 private companies to receive 20 metric tons of weapons-grade plutonium — first transfer of its kind in U.S. history

The Department of Energy announced it has selected Oklo, Standard Nuclear, SHINE Technologies, Flibe Energy, and Exodys Energy for “advanced negotiations” under the Surplus Plutonium Utilization Program. The material — enough for roughly 2,000 nuclear warheads — comes from dismantled Cold War-era weapons stored at Los Alamos, Pantex, and Savannah River. The connection to the missing scientists: Los Alamos, where three of the five missing New Mexicans worked (Casias, Chavez, and former director Charles McMillan, killed in an unexplained head-on crash in 2024), is a primary source of the surplus plutonium being transferred. Oklo’s CEO sits on PCAST, the advisory board that shaped the program. DOE Secretary Chris Wright sat on Oklo’s board until Feb 2025. And Bob Mumgaard — CEO of Commonwealth Fusion Systems, spun out of the MIT lab directed by murdered physicist Nuno Loureiro — was named to PCAST three months after Loureiro’s death. Nonproliferation experts have raised concerns about weapons-grade material entering private hands.
