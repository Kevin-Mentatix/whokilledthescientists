The Epstein Connections

$12.9M+ in science funding · institutional overlap with dead scientists · no direct links found
← Part of the "Coincidences" series

No direct Epstein-to-individual-scientist link has been established. But the institutional overlap, the shell companies obscuring the money, and the intelligence connections surrounding Epstein create a pattern that is difficult to dismiss as coincidence.

SFI personnel overlap heavily with Los Alamos National Laboratory — they share researchers, advisory boards, and social networks. Harvard — Lieber — Thousand Talents Pipeline

Feng Yanghe — the Chinese military AI scientist who died in a 2:35 AM car crash and was buried with military honors — was a Harvard visiting scholar during Lieber’s tenure as department chair (2011–2013). Epstein’s money flowed into the same department that produced a convicted Chinese intelligence asset.
Three threads converge at Harvard: Epstein’s $6.5M, Lieber’s Chinese intelligence pipeline, and Feng Yanghe’s military AI work. The funding, the espionage, and a dead scientist all pass through one department.

Tier 2 — The Shadow Infrastructure

Shell companies, intelligence ties, and obscured money flows
TIER 2
💰
Mondaloy Shell Company Network
Unknown duration
Epstein routed science funding through a network of shell companies collectively called the Mondaloy chain. These entities obscured the true source, destination, and scale of his financial support for scientific research. The $12.9M in known funding represents only what has been publicly traced — the actual total is likely significantly higher. Shell companies were registered across multiple jurisdictions, making full forensic accounting difficult. Several universities have acknowledged receiving Epstein funds they initially denied.
If the known $12.9M already overlaps with multiple institutions connected to dead scientists, the unknown funding routed through shells could extend that overlap further. We may never know the true scale.

1980s – 2019
Epstein’s network included documented intelligence-adjacent figures. Robert Maxwell (Ghislaine’s father) is widely reported to have been a Mossad asset — confirmed by multiple former intelligence officials after his 1991 death. Ehud Barak, former Israeli Prime Minister and military intelligence chief, visited Epstein’s properties and received funding through Epstein-connected entities. Leslie Wexner, Epstein’s primary financial backer, granted him sweeping power of attorney. Epstein’s own intelligence ties remain murky — former CIA officials have made conflicting statements about whether he was an asset, an informant, or simply useful.
If Epstein operated at the intersection of science funding and intelligence networks, his institutional access could have served as a mapping function — identifying which scientists worked on what, where the sensitive research was happening, and who had access to classified programs.

PROMIS Software & Signals Intelligence
1980s – 2000s

MIT Media Lab director Joi Ito resigned in September 2019 after the New Yorker revealed he had accepted Epstein funding and actively concealed the relationship. Internal emails showed Epstein’s name was marked as “Voldemort” and “he who must not be named” in MIT communications — staff knew the donations were toxic and hid them anyway. MIT’s own investigation found at least $2 million in Epstein-linked gifts. The pattern of institutional concealment raises the question: how many other institutions received Epstein money and simply never got caught?
If MIT actively concealed Epstein funding, other institutions may have done the same. The known $12.9M could be a fraction of the actual total.

The institutions at the center of this overlap — Los Alamos, Caltech, the Santa Fe Institute — share a common ancestor: the Manhattan Project. LANL was built for the bomb. Caltech supplied key physicists. SFI was founded by LANL alumni to continue interdisciplinary physics research. Epstein’s science funding targeted this lineage specifically — not random universities, but the institutions descended from America’s most consequential classified weapons program. The scientists on this site who worked at these institutions inherited that lineage and the clearances that come with it.
Epstein didn’t fund science broadly. He funded the Manhattan Project’s institutional descendants — the exact ecosystem where classified defense research and the dead scientists converge.

The Bottom Line

No direct link between Jeffrey Epstein and any individual dead or missing scientist has been established. But the institutional overlap is systemic, not incidental. Epstein’s $12.9M+ in known science funding — plus an unknown amount routed through shell companies — flowed into the exact institutions where these scientists worked: Harvard, Caltech/JPL, MIT, Santa Fe Institute, and the Los Alamos ecosystem. His intelligence-adjacent network had the means to map who worked on what. Whether this overlap is coincidence or infrastructure depends on questions that Epstein’s death in a federal detention center ensured would never be answered under oath.
